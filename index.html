Save the life of mother, save the life of a baby.

One of the biggest obstacles women face while pregnant is getting to a health facility to safely deliver.

Most people do not own a car or a motorcycle, and a majority live greater than 5 km from a health center (an hour by foot). Can you imagine trying to walk an hour on foot when you were pregnant, and in labor?

SAFE provides motorcycle ambulances to solve this problem, and Ride For Life connects mothers with drivers when they need it the most.

Who We Are

SAFE serves mothers in Uganda to improve maternal and child survival. We work with community groups to develop motorcycle ambulance programs for emergency maternal and child referral, however, sometimes it can be challenging for mothers to contact teh drivers in time.

Ride for Life solves this problem by identifying and sending a text message to the nearest trained motorcycle ambulance driver, so that he can be dispatched the mother's location as quickly as possible.

Mothers

Among the many things as a soon-to-be-mother you have to prepare, one that can't be overlooked is how you will get to a medical facility to deliver your beautiful new baby!

However, traditional transportation options may be largely inaccessible if you live in a rural area, or worse, if your baby decides to arrive during the treacherous rainy seasons!

Ride for Life is an app that connects you with trained motorcycle ambulance drivers, who will safely transport you to a medical facility when time comes for your baby's arrival.

Drivers

You can help save the lives of mothers and babies!

SAFE works with local citizens and medical professionals to develop an innovative, cost-effective solution--a comprehensive motorcycle ambulance system.

Volunteers from the community are trained in defensive driving and drive the ambulances; dispatch is handled through the Ride for Life app; and patients pay a subsidized fee to contribute to the cost of gasoline.
